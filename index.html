Market Landscape

Broward County has 169,000+ seniors 65+ across 20 key zip codes, with heavy concentration in western Broward (33321 Coral Springs: 14,099 seniors, 33322 Coral Springs: 12,949). 246 home care providers compete for market share alongside 206 Medicare-certified home health agencies. The market is fragmented — no single operator holds more than 5 locations (Sinceri Senior Living leads with 5). This creates massive opportunity for a well-connected franchise with strong corporate support.

Three Referral Channels to Build
The hospital discharge pipeline is the highest-volume channel. When a patient is leaving the hospital or finishing rehab at a SNF, someone has to arrange home care. That's the discharge planner or social worker — they're the gatekeeper. My job is to be the first person they think of. I'd start with Broward Health North, Boca Regional, and Delray Medical — those three alone cover most of the territory.
Channel 1: Hospital Discharge Pipeline
Highest volume. Patients discharged home need immediate care.
Targets:
  • Discharge planners at 5 major hospitals
  • Social workers at 10+ SNF/rehab facilities
  • Hospice liaisons (VITAS, Trustbridge)
How it works:
Patient is leaving hospital/rehab → discharge planner needs home care → they call us → caregiver starts within 48 hours

The professional network is higher value per client. Elder law attorneys are gold — families come to them stressed about Medicaid, overwhelmed by care decisions. The attorney says 'let me connect you with someone who handles home care' and that's us. There are 10 elder law firms in territory I'd target. Same with geriatricians — when a doctor sees a patient declining, they recommend home care to the family.
Channel 2: Professional Referral Network
Higher value per client. Trusted advisor relationship.
Targets:
  • Elder law attorneys (10 firms in territory)
  • Geriatricians & neurologists (7+ physicians)
  • Care managers & geriatric care consultants
How it works:
Family visits attorney for Medicaid planning → attorney says "while we work on this, here's who handles home care" → family calls us
= Private Duty + Placement Revenue
The community channel is slower to build but incredibly sticky once you're in. Broward has a huge Jewish community — the JCCs, the Jewish Family Services, the synagogues. These organizations serve thousands of seniors and their families trust them completely. When a rabbi or program director hears a member is struggling with care, their recommendation carries enormous weight. Same with the Area Agency on Aging — they're the gateway for seniors seeking any kind of service. And the 7 VA facilities are another pipeline for veterans who need home care.
Channel 3: Community & Faith-Based
Long-cycle, trust-based. Steady pipeline once established.
Targets:
  • JCCs, synagogues, churches (strong in Broward)
  • Area Agency on Aging (ADRC gateway)
  • Senior centers (Volen Center, JFS orgs)
  • VA facilities (7 locations)

90-Day Territory Strategy Here's how I'd attack the first 90 days. Month 1 is all hospitals — that's the fastest path to referrals. Discharge planners send patients home every day, and they need a reliable agency to call. Month 2, I layer in the professional network — attorneys and doctors who drive higher-value, longer-term clients. Month 3, I go deep into the community — faith-based orgs, VA, senior centers. By day 90, I'd have relationships across all three channels and a steady pipeline flowing.
Week 1: I'd get the full list of discharge planners at Broward Health North, Boca Regional, and Delray Medical. Week 2-3: face-to-face introductions with my card and a capabilities sheet. Week 4: first lunch-and-learn. At the same time, I'm visiting the top 10 SNFs — they discharge to home care daily. The goal is 15-20 discharge planner contacts by end of month 1.
